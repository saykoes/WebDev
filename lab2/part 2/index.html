Japan | 日本

Japan is an archipelago, or string of islands, on the eastern edge of Asia. There are four main islands: Hokkaido, Honshu, Shikoku, and Kyushu. There are also nearly 4,000 smaller islands! Japan's nearest mainland neighbors are the Siberian region of Russia in the north and Korea and China farther south.

Where the future meets tradition. Explore a neon-lit metropolis of towering skyscrapers, hidden shrines, and the vibrant pop-culture of Akihabara.

FAQ | よくある質問

Do I need a visa to visit Japan?

Visa requirements depend on your citizenship. Currently, citizens of many countries (including the US, UK, and EU) can enter Japan for tourism without a visa for up to 90 days. However, we always recommend checking the official website of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Japan or your local embassy before booking, as regulations can change.

When is the best time to see the Cherry Blossoms (Sakura)?

Sakura typically blooms from late March to early April in major cities like Tokyo, Kyoto, and Osaka. If you miss the peak in the south, you can still catch the blossoms in northern regions like Hokkaido as late as early May. Keep in mind that this is the most popular travel season, so we recommend booking at least 6–8 months in advance.

How do I stay connected (Internet/Phone) while traveling?

Staying connected is very easy. You can either rent a "Pocket Wi-Fi" device, which allows multiple devices to connect at once, or purchase a travel eSIM if your phone supports it. Most major airports have kiosks where you can pick up these devices upon arrival, or we can arrange to have them delivered directly to your first hotel.
